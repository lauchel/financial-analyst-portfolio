Key findings
Base case assumptions, 12% blended take rate
42%of gross revenue
The US entity is the single largest contributor to consolidated revenue, driven by GMV scale rather than FX benefit, since it carries no currency translation exposure.
~14%swing
Consolidated net revenue swings roughly 14 percent between the strong dollar and weak dollar scenarios at a fixed take rate, showing meaningful FX translation risk across the entity base.
$56Kper month
Each 1 point increase in blended take rate lifts consolidated net revenue by approximately 56 thousand dollars per month under base case FX, illustrating the operating leverage available through pricing.
2%of gross revenue
Intercompany eliminations remain a modest but necessary adjustment, underscoring why entity level reconciliation matters even at small dollar magnitudes for audit ready consolidated reporting.
