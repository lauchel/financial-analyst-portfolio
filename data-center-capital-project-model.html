Data Center Capital Project Investment Model

A capital budgeting case study for a 50MW data center development, built on real development cost and yield assumptions from Digital Realty Trust's FY2025 disclosures.

Overview
Underwriting a 50MW development

Digital infrastructure investors underwrite new data center capacity the same way any capital intensive developer underwrites a project: phased construction spend, a lease up ramp to stabilized income, and a return profile that has to clear the cost of capital before a dollar gets committed. This model rebuilds that process end to end, using Digital Realty's own disclosed development economics as the input set rather than invented numbers.

Takeaways
Recommendation & key risks
  • Base case unlevered IRR clears an 8.5% WACC with meaningful room, supporting moving to full underwriting subject to a real lender term sheet.
  • DSCR holds above 1.5x through the lease up ramp at a 55% loan to cost, giving headroom against a slower start.
  • Lease up pace is the single biggest lever on returns. A delayed ramp to stabilization compresses IRR faster than almost any other input.
  • Exit cap rate expansion erodes terminal value directly. Every 50 basis points of cap rate expansion costs a little over a point of unlevered IRR in this structure.
