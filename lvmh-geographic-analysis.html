China slowdown
Asia excl. Japan fell 12.5% in FY2024 — the sharpest regional decline. Chinese luxury consumers shifted spending to Japan and Europe, attracted by favorable exchange rates, rather than buying domestically. This explains why Japan surged +28% while mainland Asia contracted.
Japan — the yen effect
Japan's +28% growth in FY2024 was the group's standout performance, driven by a weak yen making luxury goods ~30% cheaper for foreign visitors. This is a currency arbitrage effect — Chinese tourists flocked to Tokyo to buy Louis Vuitton at a discount. Sustainable only while yen stays weak.
Americas resilience
The US held steady at ~25% of revenue with +3% growth, anchored by Tiffany & Co. and Bulgari's jewellery strength. The US market has structurally grown from 22% of revenue in FY2020 to 25% in FY2024 — a deliberate diversification away from China concentration.
Europe — tourist-driven
Europe's +2% growth masks an important dynamic: much of the spending is by Chinese and Asian tourists buying in Paris and Milan rather than local European consumers. If Chinese outbound tourism slows, European numbers face downside risk despite the region appearing stable.
