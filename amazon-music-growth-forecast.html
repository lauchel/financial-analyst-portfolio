Scenario comparison — FY2027E

Key metrics under each case

Bull case drivers
Prime bundle conversion accelerates (+3pp MAU growth). Price increases stick with no material churn. Ad market rebounds strongly. Amazon expands into 5 new markets. Operating leverage kicks in as royalty rates stabilize at scale.
Base case assumptions
Steady Prime-driven growth (8–9% YoY). Gradual paid conversion improvement. ARPU grows ~5% YoY. Ad revenue grows in line with digital ad market. Churn improves slightly from Prime lock-in effect. Consistent with FY2024 trajectory.
Bear case risks
MAU growth slows to 5–6% as Prime decelerates in mature markets. Spotify/Apple Music pressure limits conversion. Ad market weakness crimps ad-supported revenue. Churn ticks up during macro downturn as users cancel discretionary subscriptions.

Amazon Music vs competitors — market share
Streaming music paid subscriber market share estimates FY2024 (MIDiA Research)
Prime bundle advantage
Amazon Music's primary moat is Prime bundling — ~240M global Prime subscribers represent a captive acquisition funnel that Spotify and Apple Music cannot replicate. Prime members have structurally lower churn (~6.6% vs ~10% industry average) because cancellation means losing Prime benefits, not just music. This is the most important assumption in the model.
ARPU upside opportunity
At ~$72/year, Amazon Music Unlimited's paid ARPU significantly lags Spotify (~$57/year) and Apple Music (~$84/year). The gap vs Apple signals pricing power exists. A $5 ARPU increase to $77 would add ~$350M in annual revenue by FY2027 at projected subscriber levels — the highest-ROI lever in the model.
Ad revenue gap
Amazon Music's ad-supported revenue per user (~$8.50/year) lags Spotify's equivalent significantly. Despite Amazon's unrivaled first-party purchase data — the most valuable ad targeting signal in existence — the music platform has not yet monetized this advantage. Closing even half the gap with Spotify would add ~$400M in annual ad revenue.
Conversion rate plateau
At ~60% paid conversion (vs Spotify's ~39%), Amazon Music looks highly efficient — but this partially reflects that most users arrive via Prime (already committed). The free-to-paid funnel for standalone Amazon Music Unlimited sign-ups shows slower conversion, suggesting the Prime bundle is doing heavy lifting that isn't replicable in international expansion markets.
