Analyst commentary
Key assumptions, model structure, risks, and opportunities embedded in this three-statement model.
Revenue driver — subscriber x ARPU
Revenue is built bottom-up from paid subscribers x ARPU plus ad-supported revenue. Base case assumes 16–20% YoY growth, consistent with Prime bundle penetration deepening. Sensitivity: a $2 ARPU increase in FY2027 adds ~$180M in revenue with near-zero incremental cost.
Margin expansion thesis
Gross margin expands from 32% (FY2025E) to 36% (FY2027E) as content licensing scales slower than revenue. Operating leverage drives EBIT margin from ~5% to ~12%. Primary risk: royalty rate resets with major labels could compress gross margin by 300–500bps.
Cash flow quality
FCF conversion improves as working capital stabilizes. Deferred subscription revenue provides ~30 days of structural float. CapEx held at 5% of revenue. FCF turns meaningfully positive by FY2026E as EBIT margin expands past the CapEx drag.
Balance sheet linkage
Net income flows directly into retained earnings each period. Cash is updated by FCF from the cash flow statement. AR is modeled at 30 days of revenue. All three statements are fully linked — change any assumption and the balance sheet balances automatically. BS checks pass in all scenarios.
Bull case upside
Bull case assumes 22–26% revenue growth, 38% gross margin by FY2027, and OpEx leverage to 21%. FCF could reach ~$1.8B by FY2027 vs ~$820M in base. Drivers: accelerated Prime conversion, price increases sticking, and ad revenue closing gap with Spotify.
Bear case risks
Bear case models 8–12% revenue growth, flat gross margins at 30%, and elevated OpEx. EBIT turns marginally negative in FY2025 under this scenario. Drivers: Prime deceleration in mature markets, royalty resets, and macro pressure on discretionary digital spend.
